All About Apples

Interesting facts about apples

December 15, 2024 | By Johnny Appleseed | Fruits

The apple tree is deciduous, generally standing from 2 to 4.5 metres (6 to 15 feet) tall in cultivation and up to 15 m (49 ft) in the wild, though more typically 2 to 10 m (6.5 to 33 ft).[6][2] When cultivated, the size, shape and branch density are determined by rootstock selection and trimming method.[6] Apple trees may naturally have a rounded to erect crown with a dense canopy of leaves.[7] The bark of the trunk is dark gray or gray-brown, but young branches are reddish or dark-brown with a smooth texture.[2][8] Young twigs are covered in fine downy hairs; they become hairless when older.[8]

TThere are more than 7,500 cultivars of apples. Different cultivars are bred for various tastes and uses, including cooking, eating raw, and cider or apple juice production. Trees and fruit are prone to fungal, bacterial, and pest problems, which can be controlled by a number of organic and non-organic means. In 2010, the fruit's genome was sequenced as part of research on disease control and selective breeding in apple production.
